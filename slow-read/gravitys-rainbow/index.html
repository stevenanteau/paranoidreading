Gravity's Rainbow

Thomas Pynchon · A Season

The paranoid novel. A screaming comes across the sky, and for seven hundred pages Pynchon never lets it land. Rockets, cartels, Pavlovian conditioning, and a war that turns out to be a front for something worse, all watched by a man who comes apart as the plot closes in around him. It's the hardest, funniest, most rewarding book on the whole list, and the one this program is basically named after. Read it slow, read it with the annotations open, and let the paranoia do its work. Twelve weeks, one rocket's arc.

How it works. The read goes in order, one stretch at a time. The dispatch lands on the Substack and the video on the channel. Page numbers key to the standard Viking / Penguin edition, so go by the section, not the exact page.
Read with these open. Gravity's Rainbow is built to be annotated, and you shouldn't do it without help. Keep the Gravity's Rainbow Guide and the PynchonWiki page-by-page beside you and check them as you go. Every allusion, every song, every rocket is glossed there. Don't fight the book bare-handed.
